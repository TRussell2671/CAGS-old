National Security Highlights from Capitol Hill

January 12 - 16, 2015
Senate Highlights
Public Bills and Resolutions
  • January 16: Sen. Markey (D-MA) introduced S.188 to ensure that oil transported through the Keystone XL pipeline into the United States is used to reduce United States dependence on Middle Eastern oil
  • January 13: Sen. Ayotte (R-NH) introduced S.165 to extend and enhance prohibitions and limitations with respect to the transfer or release of individuals detained at United States Naval Station, Guantanamo Bay, Cuba
  • January 13: Sen. McCain (R-AZ) introduced S.159 to improve the operation of the Department of Homeland Security's Unmanned Aircraft System Program
  • January 13: Sen. Hatch (R-UT) introduced S.153 to amend the Immigration and Nationality Act to authorize additional visas for well-educated aliens to live and work in the United States
Committee Hearings
  • January 13: Select Committee on Intelligence met in closed session to receive a briefing on certain intelligence matters from officials of the intelligence community
House Highlights
Public Bills and Resolutions
  • January 16: Rep. Walorski (R-IN) introduced H.R.401 to extend and enhance prohibitions and limitations with respect to the transfer or release of individuals detained at United States Naval Station, Guantanamo Bay, Cuba
  • January 16: Rep. Rangel (D-NY) introduced H.R.403 to lift the trade embargo on Cuba
  • January 14: Rep. Clawson (R-FL) introduced H.R.364 to prohibit assistance to the Palestinian Authority until it withdraws its request to join the International Criminal Court
  • January 14: Rep. Bilirakas (R-FL) introduced H.R.361 to amend the Homeland Security Act of 2002 to codify authority under existing grant guidance authorizing use of Urban Area Security Initiative and State Homeland Security Grant Program funding for enhancing medical preparedness, medical surge capacity, and mass prophylaxis capabilities
  • January 14: Rep. Noem (R-SD) introduced H.R.350 to direct the Interagency Task Force to Monitor and Combat Trafficking to identify strategies to prevent children from becoming victims of trafficking and review trafficking prevention efforts, to protect and assist in the recovery of victims of trafficking
  • January 14: Rep. Grayson (D-FL) introduced H.R.345 to make persons who conspire to commit, commit, or benefit from an act of human trafficking ineligible for admission to the United State of America
  • January 13: Rep. Poe (R-TX) introduced H.R.300 to provide for operational control of the international border of the United States, and for other purposes; to the Committee on Homeland Security
  • January 12: Rep. Hastings (R-WA) introduced   H.R.277 , to prohibit assistance to the Palestinian Authority until it withdraws its request to join the International Criminal Court
  • January 12: Rep. Rush (D-IL) introduced H.R.274 , to lift the trade embargo on Cuba, and for other purposes; to the Committee on Foreign Affairs, and in addition to the Committees on Ways and Means, Energy and Commerce, the Judiciary, Financial Services, Oversight and Government Reform, and Agriculture
