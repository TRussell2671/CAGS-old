National Security Highlights from Capitol Hill

November 17 - 21, 2014

Due to the Thanksgiving, the National Security Highlights Newsletter will resume December 1-5, 2014.
Happy Thanksgiving!
Senate Highlights
Public Bills and Resolutions
  • November 19: Sen. Markey (D-MA) introduced S. Res. 2942, to establish a Hospital Fund for the treatment of individuals with Ebola or other specified infectious diseases
  • November 19: Sen. Feinstein (D-CA) introduced S. Res. 2941, to combat human trafficking; to the Committee on the Judiciary
  • November 18: Senate resumed consideration of S. Res. 2685, to reform the authorities of the Federal Government to require the production of certain business records, conduct electronic surveillance, use pen registers and trap and trace devices, and use other forms of information gathering for foreign intelligence, counterterrorism, and criminal purposes
  • November 17: Sen. Schumer (D-NY) introduced S. Res 2934, to prohibit the unauthorized use of electronic tracking devices; to the Committee on the Judiciary
Committee Hearings
  • November 19: Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs concluded a hearing to examine preparedness and response to public health threats, focusing on how ready we are
  • November 18: Select Committee on Intelligence held a closed hearing in regards to ongoing intelligence matters from officials in the intelligence community
House Highlights
Public Bills and Resolutions
  • November 19: Rep. Bentivolio (R-MI) introduced H. Res. 5738 , making supplemental appropriations for the Department of Homeland Security for purposes of establishing and maintaining mobile hospital units for responding to an epidemic
  • November 18: Rep. Kinzinger (R-IL) introduced  H. Res. 758 , strongly condemning the actions of the Russian Federation, under President Vladimir Putin, which has carried out a policy of aggression against neighboring countries aimed at political and economic domination
  • November 18: Rep. Stockman (R-TX) introduced H. Res. 5734 , to achieve a lasting peace in the Middle East and improve the economic situation for its people; to the Committee on Foreign Affairs
  • November 17: Rep. Brooks (R-AL) introduced H. Res. 757 , providing for authority to initiate litigation for actions by the President or other executive branch officials inconsistent with their duties under the Constitution of the United States with respect to the implementation of the immigration laws
Committee Hearings
  • November 20: Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence held a hearing entitled "Cybersecurity Threats: The Way Forward''
  • November 20: Committee on Foreign Affairs, Subcommittee on the Middle East and North Africa held a hearing entitled "Examining What a Nuclear Iran Deal Means for Global Security''
  • November 20: Committee on Foreign Affairs held a markup on the following legislative measures: H.R. 5710, the "Ebola Emergency Response Act''; H. Res. 714, reaffirming the peaceful and collaborative resolution of maritime and jurisdictional disputes in the South China Sea and the East China Sea as provided for by universally recognized principles of international law, and reaffirming the strong support of the United States Government for freedom of navigation and other internationally lawful uses of sea and airspace in the Asia-Pacific region; and H. Res. 758, strongly condemning the actions of the Russian Federation, under President Vladimir Putin, which has carried out a policy of aggression against neighboring countries aimed at political and economic domination.
  • November 19:Committee on Foreign Affairs, Subcommittee on the Middle East and North Africa held a hearing entitled "Next Steps for U.S. Foreign Policy on Syria and Iraq''
  • November 18: Committee on Foreign Affairs, Subcommittee on Europe, Eurasia, and Emerging Threats held a hearing entitled "Water Sharing Conflicts and the Threat to International Peace''. Testimony was heard from Paul Sullivan, Professor of Economics, National Defense University
  • November 18: Committee on Foreign Affairs, Subcommittee on Europe, Eurasia, and Emerging Threats held a hearing entitled "Water Sharing Conflicts and the Threat to International Peace''. Testimony was heard from Paul Sullivan, Professor of Economics, National Defense University
  • November 18: Committee on Foreign Affairs, Subcommittee on Europe, Eurasia, and Emerging Threats held a hearing entitled "Water Sharing Conflicts and the Threat to International Peace''. Testimony was heard from Paul Sullivan, Professor of Economics, National Defense University
  • November 18: Committee on Foreign Affairs, Subcommittee on Terrorism, Nonproliferation, and Trade held a hearing entitled "Iranian Nuclear Talks: Negotiating a Bad Deal?''
  • November 18: Committee on Foreign Affairs, Subcommittee on Africa, Global Health, Global Human Rights, and International Organizations held a hearing entitled "Fighting Ebola: A Ground-Level View''
  • November 18: Committee on Energy and Commerce held a hearing entitled "Update on the U.S. Public Health Response to the Ebola Outbreak''
  • November 17: Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence held a closed hearing entitled "Ongoing Intelligence Activities"
