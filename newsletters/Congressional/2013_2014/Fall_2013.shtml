September 18: H. R. 301: to provide for the establishment of the Special Envoy to Promote Religious Freedom of Religious Minorities in the Near East and South Central Asia, was passed by the House on 18 September 2013. The envoy would act as an extension of and work under the auspices of the Department of State to monitor and protect religious minorities from acts of intolerance and incitement. Further, the envoy would prioritize programs and activities in Egypt, Iran, Iraq, Afghanistan, and Pakistan. The bill has been sent to the Senate for further approval.

September 17: S.793: Organization of American States Revitalization and Reform Act of 2013, initially introduced by Senator Robert Menendez (D-NJ) on April 2013, passed the Senate in July 2013, passed the House with changes on 17 September 2013. The bill expresses the sense of Congress that the Organization of American States (OAS) should be the primary multi-lateral diplomatic entity for regional dispute resolution and promotion of democratic governance. It would require the Secretary of State to develop a multiyear strategy to bolster the OAS and improve its processes for managing both budget and personnel.
