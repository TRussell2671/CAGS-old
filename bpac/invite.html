The U.S. Consulate General Kolkata, in partnership with the Center on American and Global Security at Indiana University, Bloomington and the Observer Research Foundation, is pleased to invite you to attend Building Pan-Asian Connectivity Conference.

This two-day international policy conference is dedicated to highlighting the most recent scholarship on India’s ties to Southeast Asia and East Asia in the domains of security, trade, and environmental affairs with special attention on water management. Held at the Oberoi Grand Hotel in Kolkata, India on March 10-11, 2015, the conference brings together business, academic, government, and civilian experts from the United States, India, and greater Asia who are leading authorities in their fields to examine the opportunities and obstacles in developing policies that enhance regional connectivity between India and its neighbors. The U.S. Indo-Pacific Economic Corridor will be a major policy focus of the conference, as well as an analysis of India’s “Look/Act East” policy.

We are expecting U.S. Ambassador to India, Richard Verma, to attend the conference. This conference embodies President Obama and PM Modi’s vision of fostering a relationship between the U.S. and India for enhanced economic growth and stability in this region.

The conference consists of three sessions dedicated to security, trade, and the environment. For information regarding these sessions, please visit the Building Pan-Asian Connectivity Conference website.
