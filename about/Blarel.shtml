Nicolas Blarel

Nicolas Blarel is a doctoral student at Indiana University, Bloomington. Prior to joining IU, he received a M.A. in Comparative Politics from Sciences-Po in Paris in 2006. Nicolas specializes in International Relations, with a more specific focus on security issues in South Asia. Nicolas has been particularly analyzing India's relations with the Middle-East, and especially with Israel. Nicolas has published his master thesis on Indo-Israeli strategic relations (Inde et Israël: le rapprochement stratégique, pragmatisme et complémentarité (Paris: L’Harmattan, 2006) and has a forthcoming book on this topic: Continuity, Change and Compromise in India’s Foreign Policy: Explaining India’s Israel Policy from 1922 to Today.

Nicolas has equally published book chapters and articles on India's nuclear policies, and more specifically on terrorism and nuclear escalation; India's relations with the U.S.; the evaluation of India’s soft power potential; and India's insurgent movements and state-making. Nicolas has worked for the French Foreign Ministry’s policy planning staff (the Centre d’Analyses et de Prévisions) on questions related to Afghanistan, South Asia, and nuclear proliferation. Nicolas has also been a visiting fellow at the Institute for Defence Studies and Analyses (IDSA) in New Delhi. Last year, he was editorial assistant at the peer-reviewed academic journal, International Studies Quarterly.
