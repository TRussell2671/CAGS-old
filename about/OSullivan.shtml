Piper O'Sullivan

Piper O’Sullivan is a Ph.D. student in the Department of Central Eurasian Studies with a major in Iranian Studies and a minor in Near Eastern Languages and Cultures. Her research interests include Conflict Studies and the intersection between literature and politics in Afghanistan, Iran, and Northwest Pakistan. Her M.A. Thesis in Central Eurasian Studies (2012) analyzed modern Pashto poetry and the development of neo-Taliban propaganda. She has completed advanced linguistic training in Pashto and Persian—having been the recipient of several Foreign Language and Area Studies scholarships as well as awarded the Critical Language Scholarship in Tajikistan. She holds a B.A. from Mount Holyoke College.
