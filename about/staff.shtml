Staff List

CAGS employs a dedicated staff that oversees research, conferences, symposia, guest speakers, and coordination with other units at Indiana University. Next to each team member below, you will find their biography and a summary of their roles and responsibilities at CAGS

Sumit Ganguly

Director

Sumit Ganguly is a Professor of Political Science, holds the Rabindranath Tagore Chair in Indian Cultures and Civilizations and directs the Center on American and Global Security at Indiana University, Bloomington. He has previously taught at James Madison College of Michigan State University, Hunter College and The Graduate Center of the City University of New York and the University of Texas at Austin.

Brea Bailey

Associate Director

Brea Bailey is a 2014 graduate of Berea College, located in Berea, Kentucky, where she received her Bachelor’s Degrees in Communication and Peace Studies. After college, Brea moved to Washington, D.C. where she began her Master’s of Arts at American University. In her previous experiences, she has worked with varying projects to foster global partnerships in the areas of conflict studies, education, and intercultural understanding; she has served the Center on American and Global Security as the Associate Director since July 2015. In her current role, she most enjoys collaborating with students in responding to, thinking critically of, and engaging with current global trends and issues.

Brandon Miliate

Graduate Assistant

Brandon J. Miliate is a third year graduate student in the Department of Political Science. He studies the use of autonomy and ethno-federalism as a conflict resolution mechanism in ethno-national intrastate conflict in Northeast India, Bangladesh, and Myanmar. Previously, Brandon researched small state foreign policy, specializing in Mongolian national security. He is well versed in ethnographic and historical methods as well as geospatial analysis. His language abilities are fairly broad with substantive knowledge of Mongolian, Russian, Bengali, and Burmese as well as some limited Persian (Farsi). Brandon plans on finishing his PhD in 2018, and begin his career in academia.

Tyler Russell

Web Developer

Tyler Russell is a senior majoring in Near Eastern Languages and Cultures and minoring in Informatics. He obtained his Associate of Arts in Arabic from the Defense Language Institute in 2012. His academic focuses include security in the Middle East, Islam, cyber-security, and application development. In the future he hopes to combine his knowledge of technology and the Middle East, in order to implement technological solutions in Middle Eastern businesses. Tyler currently serves as the Web Developer for the Center and assists in design and implementation of web products. In addition to his work at CAGS, Tyler is a Peer Advisor for veterans. He is a veteran of the United States Marine Corps and a member of the Student Veterans of America.

Justice Eiden

Program Assistant

Justice Eiden serves the Center as its Program Assistant, a Federal Work Study position new to CAGS. Justice is a sophomore currently majoring in Political Science with prospective minors in both Economics and International Studies. His research interests in the department include American domestic security, Middle Eastern security, and global institutions that exist to create better regional and global peace and stability. When not in class or working with CAGS, Justice campaigns for Democratic candidates, serves as the Co-Chair for the IU Students for Bernie Sanders Organization, travels across the country with IU’s nationally-acclaimed Model United Nations Team, reports news coverage for WIUX Student Radio News, and participates in No Labels, a Bi-Partisan political club that exists to facilitate conversation and create compromise with both Democrats in Republicans around the country.

Jack Renner

Research Intern

Jack is a second year MPA student at IU’s School of Public and Environmental Affairs (SPEA), concentrating in Foreign Policy and Security in addition to Public Financial Administration. Jack has interned with the U.S. Navy Bureau of Medicine and Surgery, the National Democratic Institute for International Affairs, and the U.S. House Republican Policy Committee. With CAGS since January 2014, he focuses primarily on conflicts in the Middle East and North Africa as well as European and U.S. security issues. His undergraduate honors thesis analyzed and compared the international community’s responses to “Arab Spring” crises in Libya and Syria through the context of the “Responsibility to Protect” and great power politics. Jack received a Bachelor’s degree from SPEA majoring in Policy Analysis in 2015 and will complete his Master’s this May. He plans to pursue a career in governmental affairs and security policy in the Washington D.C. area.

John McHugh

Research Intern

A sophomore Wells Scholar in IU's School of Global and International Studies, John McHugh is an East Asian Languages and Cultures major with minors in Central Eurasian Studies and History. His studies focus on China, with special emphasis on how the country's foreign, economic, and minority policies intersect with its evolving role in Central Asia.  Through State Department Critical Language and NSLI-Y Scholarships, John has participated in language immersion programs in Suzhou and Changchun. As a Reischauer Scholar, John was selected to present his research at Stanford University on the failures of the Japanese nuclear energy bureaucracy during the Fukushima disaster. As a Research Intern for the Center on American and Global Security, he will be assisting with research regarding pressing issues in China and the region, conducting interviews with hosted scholars, and offering logistical support for the Center’s event offerings.
