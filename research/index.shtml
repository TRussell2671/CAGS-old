Naxalite Violence

Since 1967, India has incurred thousands of violent incidents in rural areas conducted by an assortment of Maoist groups known collectively as the Naxalite movement.  This movement cites rural poverty and exploitation as motives for its actions, while critics claim that its followers are truly motivated by the potential profits to be made off of extortion.  To investigate the role of economic and social under-development in fueling Naxalite activity, Professors Sumit Ganguly, Sameeksha Desai, and Arvind Verma have undertaken a project that will employ Global Information System (GIS) software to map the types and frequency of Naxalite incidents against local developmental conditions for the period 2005-2012.

Creative Espionage Operations, Military Deceptions and Police Stings: Can you Teach Someone To Become More Creative

This research project, conducted by Professor Gene Coyle, is gathering historical examples from the espionage, military and police worlds where the operation was particularly imaginative, and to the extent possible, a look at who were the people who came up with such ideas.  The goal is to see if there is a common thread among the creators of such creative "scams", and finally to look at the question of whether you can teach people to be more creative than they were at birth.
