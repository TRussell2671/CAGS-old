"Jihad as a Grand Strategy: Islamist Militancy, National Security, and the Pakistani State"

Presenter: Paul Kapur, Naval Postgraduate School

Date: 10-26-2015

On October 26, the Center on American and Global Security hosted a private event featuring Paul Kapur, Professor in the Department of National Security Affairs at the U.S. Naval Postgraduate School. Dr. Kapur spoke on the topic “Jihad as a Grand Strategy: Islamist Militancy, National Security, and the Pakistani State”. In his lecture, he discussed why a militant proxy strategy could be attractive to a state like Pakistan. Militants are inexpensive and need no basic training, no sophisticated equipment, and no personal support or benefits. However, Dr. Kapur explains that the use of Jihad is a paradox. When a state it weak, it makes militant strategy both useful and dangerous.

"The Eyes of Islam are Watching: Transcolonial Activism in India and the Palestine Mandate, 1920-1948"

Date: 10-02-2015
